Introduction

This is a Practice Data Story for my Crash data problem, Problem 1B. It includes elements from our build 5C.

The question that we chose to answer was “Which Hartford corridors have had the most crashes, by type, in recent years?” This question is important to many people in the Hartford community. To Trinity students, just last spring a student was killed due to reckless driving along Hartford corridors (simply; streets, roads, avenues), so this issue hits them on a personal level. To the greater Hartford community, it is important to display this information in a meaningful way to make people more aware about which corridors are more dangerous and then identifying those dangerous corridors in order to find solutions to make them safer. A town not too far from Hartford, in fact it was West Hartford, had citizens voicing their opinions about the traffic safety of the town to their town hall before putting enough pressure to force more traffic measures like adding accesible pedestrain signals and pledging money to study the traffic data more in depthly. You can read more about the incident that lead to this change and the steps the people took to voice their concerns 'I’m going to die right now': Why West Hartford pedestrians feel unsafe and how town is responding.

Simply looking at a map showing the locations of crashes in Hartford may give you a partial view into where crashes occur most frequently. A general map of the crashes isn’t necessarily a bad thing, but it does not tell an accurate or complete story. Using data from the UConn Connecticut Crash Data Repository, we are using the police crash reports from 2018-2022 to take that dimensional, spatial data of crash occurrences and assign them to their respective streets. From there, we can look at the amount of crashes on a specific street and then make stronger relationships for the data like percentages of a designated injury type on certain streets and crashes when adjusted for corridor length (distance of entire corridor).

Major Observations

Major Observation 1: Main St. has the most crashes out of any street, but may not be the most dangerous.

From the initial data gathering, Main Street in Hartford had the highest number of crashes and crash related injuries from 2018-2022, with 1,153 individual incidents and 387 injuries. Yet, does this mean that Main Street is the most dangerous corridor for crashes? To answer that question we need to put our data into context. Right off the bat you may notice that the name “Main Street” denotes its centrality in Hartford, likely with high volumes of traffic and activity. This can be seen by Figure 1 below which highlights the stark number of crashes by injury type that occur in Main Street alone vs every other corrdor in Hartford. This difference is eye-popping but we will see later that while this chart is true, it is also very misleading and not the entire story.

Figure 1: Explore the Hartford Total Crashes & Injuries by Street and notice the patterns throughout it. As mentioned before, Main Street has by far the most crashes and is the only corridor with over 1,000 crashes. We can also notice that for every street, a majority of their crashes involved no injuries and almost no injuries were fatal. We can now expand on these observations and see if we normalize the data, will Main Steet still be the most dangerous corridor?
